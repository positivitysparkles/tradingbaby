## Card 01 — The 6 Entry Conditions
**The Rocket Launch**
NASA doesn't launch a rocket with 5 out of 6 systems green. One red light and the whole mission is scrubbed — no arguments, no "it's probably fine." You are mission control. All 6 conditions are your launch checklist. The moment you start saying "well, 5 out of 6 is pretty good" is the moment you blow up the rocket. Every. Light. Green. Or you don't trade.

## Card 02 — Stoch RSI
**The Rubber Ball Underwater**
Picture a rubber ball being pushed underwater. The harder you push it down — the lower K gets — the more explosive the pop back up will be. When K is at 0–10, that ball is fully submerged, held down by sellers. The second it starts rising and crosses the 20 line above D, it's launching. You're not catching the bottom — you're riding the pop. The deeper it went, the bigger the bounce.

## Card 03 — ZLSMA (The Yellow Line)
**The Glass Floor**
The yellow ZLSMA line is the floor of a glass skyscraper. Above it, you're safe — great view, trade freely, the building is solid. The moment price drops below it, you've fallen through the floor. There is nothing holding you up. W118 has a strict rule: never trade below the yellow line. Not "try to avoid it." Never. It's not a guideline — it's the foundation of the entire building.

## Card 04 — Pattern A: The Fade
**The Party That Ran Out of Food**
Someone throws a huge party and everyone shows up at 9am (the open spike). But by noon, people realize the food ran out — the catalyst was weak, the move was fake. By closing time the host is alone, cleaning up, price sitting below the ZLSMA. Don't show up the next morning hoping for leftovers. The kitchen is empty. The party is over. Move on to the next party.

## Card 05 — Pattern B: Multi-Day Runner
**The Marathon Runner**
Think of a marathon runner who sprints hard on day 1, then slows to a jog — but never stops, never sits down. That's the multi-day runner: it pulled back, but it's still running, still holding above the ZLSMA. It's catching its breath (Stoch RSI resetting overnight). Come morning, it sprints again. The key signal: still jogging above the yellow line. The moment it sits down below ZLSMA? Race over. Walk away.

## Card 06 — Pattern C: Late Accumulation
**The Clearance Sale**
Stock opened at $1.20, sold off all day to $0.65 — it's on clearance now. Then in the afternoon you notice someone quietly loading up their cart in the back of the store. No announcement, no excitement — just steady buying, Stoch RSI hitting near zero, W118 BUY quietly fires. That person knows something. The store is restocking overnight. Tomorrow morning the price tag goes back to full retail. Follow them into the store.

## Card 07 — Exit Rules
**The Road Trip With 4 Exits**
You're on a highway road trip. Exit 1 is the emergency exit at -8% — miss it and you could drive off a cliff. Exits T1, T2, T3 are rest stops where you bank profit, refuel, and let the car keep driving lighter. Most traders blow past Exit 1 saying "it'll turn around." Sometimes it does. Usually it doesn't. The road trip only works if you always, always take Emergency Exit 1 when the sign appears.

## Card 08 — The Stop Loss
**The Airbag**
Your -8% stop is the airbag in your car. You hope you never need it, but you would never — not even once — drive without one. The cost of the airbag: a small sting and you're back on the road in 10 minutes. The cost of no airbag: one crash wipes out weeks of gains and you're in the hospital. Today's YMAT lesson: entered at $1.79 without a stop. The airbag at $1.65 would have saved $2. Holding cost $4+. Wear your seatbelt.

## Card 09 — The Re-Entry Rule
**The Diving Board**
A great dive requires walking all the way to the end of the board (K drops fully below 20 — not "near" 20, not "approaching" 20 — fully below). You bounce once at the tip (the curl). Then you launch (K crosses up through 20 and passes D). If you jump from the middle of the board — K at 30, "close enough" — you get a weak, flat dive with no pop. Always walk to the end of the board. Full reset. Every single time.

## Card 10 — Session Times
**The Restaurant**
Think of the market like a restaurant. Breakfast (4am–10am ET) is when the kitchen is fresh, the chef is focused, ingredients are hot — best meal of the day, order confidently. The lunch rush (10:30am–3pm) is chaotic: kitchen overwhelmed, orders get mixed up, food arrives cold. That's midday chop — fake signals, exhausted momentum, terrible risk/reward. Don't order during the chaos. Come hungry at 4am. Leave by 10:30am.

## Card 11 — Don't Chase
**Missing the Bus**
You watch the bus pull away from the stop. You could sprint and maybe catch it — but you'd arrive out of breath, having paid the same fare as everyone who waited calmly. W118 BUY fires at $1.37. You enter at $1.79. You sprinted 30% to catch a moving bus and still paid full price. Here's the thing: the next bus — the re-entry — comes in 20 minutes. Wait at the stop. Calm. Rested. Ready.

## Card 12 — Relative Volume
**Concert Crowd Size**
Relative volume is the crowd at a concert. A 1.5x show is barely sold out — minimum energy, minimum move. A 10x show has people standing outside trying to get in — real momentum building. An 87x show (like UZX today) means the whole city showed up, news vans are parked outside, everyone in town knows. The bigger the crowd, the bigger the energy, the bigger the move. Empty venues don't make music. Don't trade empty venues.

## Card 13 — The A+ Setup
**The Perfect Storm**
Nine weather systems all collide at once: Stoch RSI came from below 10 (not just 20), K is way above D, SHA candle is massive and green, ZLSMA is curving up like a launch ramp, volume is 10x+, the catalyst is a real FDA win or merger, it's premarket hours, float is under 10M, and the signal fires on both 1m AND 5m simultaneously. This is the trade you were born to take. Full size. No hesitation. Hold through T1, T2, aim for T3 and let it breathe. UGRO did +692% on one of these. They happen 2–5 times every week. Be ready.
