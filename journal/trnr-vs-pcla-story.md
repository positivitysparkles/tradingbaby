## Think of the Stock Market Like a Party

Every morning, hundreds of stocks show up to the party.
Most are boring — they stand in the corner, barely moving, nobody cares.

But every now and then, **one stock gets a reason to run.**
Maybe news dropped. Maybe someone posted about it online. Maybe a huge buyer showed up.

That stock walks in the door and **the room turns to look.**

The question for us as traders is simple:
> **Is this stock just arriving at the party — or is it already leaving?**

That's exactly what TRNR and PCLA were showing us on May 21.

## TRNR — The Guest Who Already Peaked

### The Story

Imagine TRNR is a guy who showed up to the party at 2:10pm.

He walked in, got excited, did a little dance, jumped up on a table —
and for about 10 minutes, **everyone was watching him.**

The stock went from **$1.00 → $1.60** in one big spike. That's a 60% move in minutes.
People were buying, volume was through the roof, the music was loud.

**But then… the energy faded.**

He got off the table. People stopped watching.
The crowd drifted away. He started walking toward the door.

By the time we looked at the chart, **he was at $1.28 and heading out.**

### What the Indicators Were Saying

**The Stochastic (5m chart) — think of this as the "crowd energy meter"**

- When the K line (blue) shot up to **92**, that was the peak of the crowd cheering.
  92 means: overbought, too many people already in, nowhere left to go but down.
- By the time we checked? K was at **47 and falling.**
  The crowd was thinning. Half the room had already left.
- The K line was **above** the D line (orange) — barely. Not a strong signal.
  Think of K as the lead singer and D as the backup singer.
  When K drops below D, the lead singer walked off stage.

**The MACD — think of this as the "engine power" gauge**

- The green bars on the MACD represent momentum going forward.
- On TRNR's chart, those bars had **turned red** — the engine was reversing.
- That's like hearing the car go from accelerating to braking. You don't buy a car mid-brake.

**The Supertrend — think of this as a "traffic light"**

- Green dot below price = green light, trend is up.
- Red dot above price = red light, trend is down.
- TRNR's Supertrend had **flipped red** after the spike. Full stop sign.

**The 1-minute chart — "is there any last bounce coming?"**

- On the 1m chart, K had dropped to **26.50**, just barely above D at **17.38**.
- Low K *can* mean oversold — sometimes that means a small bounce is coming.
- But here's the trap: the **5m MACD was negative** (the bigger picture was bearish).
  Even if there's a tiny bounce on the 1-minute, the 5-minute trend is pulling it back down.
- This is called a **dead-cat bounce** — the cat's not alive, it just bounced off the floor.

### The Lesson from TRNR

> The spike already happened. You missed it. That's okay — **missing a trade is free.**
> Chasing it after the peak is how you lose money.
> 
> Wait for TRNR to fully reset — K needs to drop all the way back below 10,
> then curl back UP through 20. That's the re-entry signal. Not before.

**Rule: If you didn't buy the curl, don't buy the fade.**

## The Three Questions to Ask Every Time

Before you touch any stock, ask:

### 1. "Did I catch it at the curl — or am I chasing the spike?"
- Curl = K crossing UP through 20 from below. **This is the entry.**
- Spike = price already ran 30-60%+. **This is too late.**
- TRNR at 14:30? Too late. PCLA at 03:50am? That was the curl.

### 2. "What is the Stoch doing — building up or winding down?"
- K rising from near-zero toward 20 and crossing up = building up = **BUY signal**
- K falling from 80+ toward 50 toward 20 = winding down = **exit signal or stay out**
- TRNR: K was winding down (92 → 47 → falling)
- PCLA: K was resting (not winding down to zero yet, still above D)

### 3. "Is the MACD still green, or has it turned red?"
- Green histogram bars and growing = engine running = hold or buy
- Red histogram bars = engine reversing = sell or stay out
- TRNR: red bars on 5m = sell signal
- PCLA: green bars on 5m = still alive

## One More Thing: Why These Indicators Together?

Think of it like three witnesses at a crime scene.
You don't convict on one witness alone. You need all three to agree.

| Indicator | What it confirms |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Stoch RSI (K/D)** | Is momentum just starting? (K crossing 20 from near-zero) |
| **Smoothed HA candle** | Is the current candle green? (buyers in control right now) |
| **ZLSMA-50** | Is the overall trend still up? (price above the yellow line) |

When all three say YES at the same moment → **that's the signal.**
When any one says NO → **wait.**
